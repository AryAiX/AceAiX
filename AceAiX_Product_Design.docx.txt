1. Document Purpose & Scope
This document defines the complete user taxonomy, platform sitemap, and feature specifications for AceAiX — an AI-native athlete intelligence platform designed for the UAE and broader GCC sports market. It serves as the master reference for product architecture, UX design, and engineering specification, and is the product-design counterpart to the AceAiX investment proposal.
The document covers:
* All user roles and sub-roles within the AceAiX ecosystem
* Guest (unauthenticated) features accessible without an account
* Authenticated user dashboards and feature sets per role
* Platform sitemap and navigation hierarchy
* Core user flows for key journeys
* AI agent capabilities embedded across the platform
* Technical architecture, data model, integrations, and non-functional requirements
* A phased delivery roadmap aligned to the lean MVP-first deployment plan
AceAiX unifies three capabilities into a single ecosystem: verified medical intelligence (tamper-evident records sourced directly from licensed sports-medicine clinics), AI performance and match analytics, and a professional sports network with third-party endorsement. The product competes on intelligence rather than subjective observation — turning athletic performance and medical data into a structured, verifiable, and forecastable athlete portfolio.
2. User Role Taxonomy
AceAiX serves multiple distinct stakeholder types across the talent lifecycle. User roles are grouped into three tiers: Guest (unauthenticated), Platform Users (with registered accounts), and System Administrators.
Federations and clubs are full platform users with role-specific dashboards. Subscription packages and licensing tiers are managed through the Admin Panel by AceAiX administrators and surfaced to athletes, scouts, and clubs at sign-up and upgrade.

	6. Data Privacy, Consent & Compliance Framework
AceAiX operates under a consent-first architecture. Because the platform handles medical data sourced from licensed clinics, it aligns with UAE Federal data-protection law and applicable health-data requirements, in addition to general platform privacy obligations and minor-safeguarding standards.
6.1 Core Consent Principles
* Athletes control who can view medical data, contact details, and full performance records through granular privacy settings.
* Medical-clearance and injury data are visible to scouts and clubs only with the athlete's explicit, revocable consent.
* Minor athletes are managed under a Guardian account; direct adult-to-minor contact is mediated and safeguarded.
* Verified medical records are uploaded only by licensed partner clinics, never self-reported, and carry provenance metadata.
* Guest discovery sessions are session-scoped; search context is retained only if the user creates an account.
* Athletes can revoke any party's access at any time and can view a log of who has accessed their records.
6.2 Data Residency & Security
* Personal and medical data are stored in cloud infrastructure provisioned to meet UAE data-residency requirements for the categories that require it.
* End-to-end encryption for data in transit and at rest.
* Role-based access control (RBAC) enforced at the database level via row-level security.
* Medical records are cryptographically hashed and anchored to a provenance ledger, making any tampering detectable.
* Regular third-party security audits and periodic re-verification of partner clinics.
6.3 AI Transparency
* AI scores, forecasts, and risk summaries are labeled as AI-generated and are advisory — final recruitment and medical decisions rest with qualified humans.
* Injury-risk outputs are decision-support signals, not clinical diagnoses, and are framed as such across the interface.
* Athletes can view a log of AI interactions and scores involving their data, with a plain-language rationale.

	8. Technical Architecture
This section defines the system architecture, technology stack, deployment model, and key technical decisions underpinning the AceAiX platform. The stack mirrors the AryAiX platform standard for consistency across products.
8.1 System Architecture Overview
AceAiX follows a three-tier architecture with a dedicated media pipeline:
* Presentation Tier — single-page web application plus mobile apps, served as static assets via CDN.
* Application Tier — Supabase (managed Postgres, Auth, Edge Functions, Realtime, Storage) as the backend-as-a-service layer, supplemented by dedicated AI and media-processing services.
* Data Tier — PostgreSQL for relational data; object storage for media; a vector store for AI embeddings; and a provenance anchoring layer for verified medical records.

8.4 Multi-Tenancy Model
AceAiX uses a shared-database, row-level-isolation model:
* Clubs and federations share a single PostgreSQL database; each organization has a unique organization_id.
* PostgreSQL Row-Level Security (RLS) enforces that organization seats see only their organization's recruitment data.
* Athletes see and control only their own data; scouts see athlete data per consent and public-visibility settings.
* Super Admins bypass organization scoping.
8.5 Security Architecture
* Authentication: Supabase Auth with email/password + SMS OTP; UAE Pass for national identity verification.
* Authorization: PostgreSQL RLS enforces role- and consent-based access at the database level.
* Encryption: TLS 1.3 in transit; AES-256 at rest.
* Provenance: verified medical records are hashed and anchored; any modification is detectable on audit.
* Audit trail: all data mutations logged to an immutable audit_logs table (user, action, table, old/new value, timestamp, IP).
* File security: signed URLs for media and medical documents; no public bucket access for protected content.
* AI data handling: data sent to AI APIs is ephemeral and not used for model training; enterprise agreements with data-processing addenda; identifiers pseudonymized where feasible.
9. Data Model & Schema Design
This section defines the canonical data model for AceAiX and establishes the single source of truth for database design.
9.1 Design Principles
1. Single users table (Supabase auth.users) as the identity anchor; role-specific data lives in extension tables joined via user_id.
2. Role-based extension tables: athlete_profiles, scout_profiles, coach_profiles, medical_partner_profiles, federation_profiles, org_admin_profiles.
3. Organization as a first-class entity: organizations represents clubs and federations; seats are linked via organization_staff.
4. Immutable audit log: every mutation is logged. Medical and clearance data are never hard-deleted (soft-delete with is_deleted + deleted_at).
5. Verified records carry provenance metadata (hash, anchor reference, issuing partner) and cannot be edited in place.
6. UUID primary keys throughout; created_at / updated_at on every table (trigger-managed).

	10. Integration Specifications
This section defines all external system integrations, their purpose, protocols, and implementation approach.
10.1 UAE Pass (National Digital Identity)
Purpose: securely verify the identity of UAE residents for registration, organization onboarding, and medical-verification identity checks.
* Protocol: official UAE Pass API via OAuth 2.0 / OpenID Connect, integrated as a custom provider through Supabase Auth.
* On success, the platform receives the verified Emirates ID number, full name, nationality, and date of birth.
* Usage: optional fast-track athlete registration; mandatory verification for medical partners and organization admins; identity match at medical verification.
* The verified Emirates ID is stored encrypted in user_profiles.emirates_id; no UAE Pass tokens are persisted beyond the session.
* Fallback: manual Emirates ID upload with AI-assisted OCR verification when UAE Pass is unavailable.
10.2 Medical Partner Integration & Provenance
Goal: ingest verified medical assessments from licensed sports-medicine clinics and make them tamper-evident.
* Partners submit structured assessments, imaging, and clearances through the partner portal or a partner API.
* Each record is hashed (e.g., SHA-256) and the hash is anchored to a provenance ledger, producing a verifiable, immutable fingerprint.
* Verification of any record re-computes the hash and checks it against the anchored value; mismatches flag tampering.
* Clearance status, effective dates, and issuing partner are recorded and surfaced as badges subject to athlete consent.
* Partner license/accreditation is verified at onboarding and re-verified on a scheduled basis.

14.1 Authentication Zone (Public)
Screen / Flow
	Summary of Functionality
	Login
	Email/password, mobile OTP, and (Phase 3) UAE Pass. Links to Register and Forgot Password.
	Register
	Multi-step: method → OTP → role → core profile → optional uploads → dashboard.
	Forgot Password
	Email-based reset link to a new-password form.
	OTP Verification
	Standardized OTP entry across login, registration, and reset.
	Onboarding Wizard (Athlete)
	Portfolio setup: attributes, first highlight, identity and (optional) verification request.
	Onboarding Wizard (Recruiter/Partner)
	Credentials, affiliation/seats, and (partners) license upload; requires review.

Feature
	Key Components
	Notification Center
	Chronological feed of scout views, endorsements, messages, opportunities, and system alerts; filter and mark-as-read.
	Media Player
	Adaptive-bitrate playback with AI-tagged timeline markers; share and visibility controls.
	14.3 Error & System Pages
Page
	Description
	404 Not Found
	Friendly error with search and links to common pages, on-brand.
	500 Server Error
	Apologetic page with retry and a link to system status.
	Access Denied
	Shown when content is outside the user's role permissions; explains and links back to the dashboard.
	Maintenance
	Scheduled-downtime notice with expected return time and auto-refresh.
